**Compare Gouraud Shading and Phong Shading**

**1. Concept**

- **Gouraud shading:** computes illumination at each *vertex*, then *interpolates the color* across the polygon surface.
- **Phong shading:** interpolates the *surface normal* across the polygon, then computes illumination at *every pixel* using the interpolated normal.

**2. Steps**

*Gouraud:*
- Step 1: Compute vertex normals (average of surrounding face normals).
- Step 2: Apply illumination model at each vertex to get vertex colors.
- Step 3: Bilinearly interpolate colors along scan lines to fill the polygon.

*Phong shading:*
- Step 1: Compute vertex normals (same as Gouraud).
- Step 2: Linearly interpolate the normals across pixels: $N_{n} = N_{n-1} + \Delta N$
- Step 3: Apply the illumination model at **every pixel** using the interpolated normal.

**3. Both use the Phong illumination model:**

$$I = k_a I_a + k_d I_{\text{light}}(\mathbf{N} \cdot \mathbf{L}) + k_s I_{\text{light}}(\mathbf{R} \cdot \mathbf{V})^n$$

The difference is *where* **N** is evaluated: at vertices (Gouraud) vs. at every pixel (Phong shading).

**4. Performance comparison**

| Criterion | Gouraud | Phong Shading |
|-----------|---------|--------------|
| Computation | Per vertex (fast) | Per pixel (slow) |
| Specular highlights | May be missed or distorted | Captured accurately |
| Visual quality | Medium | High |

**5. Conclusion**

Gouraud shading is faster but can miss specular highlights between vertices. Phong shading is more expensive but produces accurate highlights, making it better for shiny surfaces.
