**Q1.** In computer graphics, we use the cross product of two vectors to calculate:
→ **c) Normal vector at the specific point on one surface**
*(Cross product of two tangent vectors on a surface gives the surface normal — used in shading.)*

---

**Q2.** In the scaling operator of 2D transformation, the fixed point is utilized for the purpose of:
→ **c) Scaling up and displaying one specified region of object in the viewport**
*(Fixed-point scaling keeps the chosen point stationary while scaling around it — effectively zooming into a specific region.)*

---

**Q3.** Why do we need homogeneous coordinates in computer graphics?
→ **a) Because it is useful for composite transformation**
*(Homogeneous coordinates allow translation to be expressed as matrix multiplication, enabling all transformations to be composed by a single matrix product.)*

---

**Q4.** The inverse transformation of a rotation denoted by matrix A in 2D is:
→ **d) All above answers are correct**
*(A rotation matrix is: square (a), its inverse equals its transpose since it is orthogonal (b), and is by definition an inverse matrix (c).)*

**Q5.** What does this row-matrix transformation do?
$$\begin{pmatrix}0 & -1 & 0\\ 1 & 0 & 0\\ 1 & 1 & 1\end{pmatrix}$$
→ **d) rotation, then translation**
*(The 2×2 top-left block $\begin{pmatrix}0&-1\\1&0\end{pmatrix}$ is a 90° rotation; the last row $(1,1,1)$ adds translation $(1,1)$ after the rotation.)*

---

**Q6.** Which 2D transformations are affine transformations?
→ **d) a), b), and c) are correct**
*(All basic 2D transformations — rotation, scaling, shear, translation, reflection — are affine.)*

**Q17.** The HSR result shows surfaces that cannot be hidden correctly (cat still has visible back faces). Which HSR method was applied?

**Q18.** What is the similar point between Phong shading and Gouraud shading?

**Q19.** To fill color in a 3D non-convex object without mismatch, which HL/HSR method applies?

**Q21.** Which projection does NOT have the direction of projection perpendicular to the viewing plane?

**Q22.** The correct order of processes to build one image of a 3D object is:
